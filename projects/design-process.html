01 · Overview

Designing with research, empathy, and iteration

This project outlines my design process and philosophy, focusing on how I approach design challenges through research, empathy, and iterative development. It highlights the importance of understanding user needs, exploring multiple solutions, and refining ideas through feedback.

The assignment asked me to design and explain my ideal user experience process rather than a specific product. Through this work, I defined how I approach problem identification, user involvement, prototyping, stakeholder collaboration, and iterative evaluation.

03 · My Design Process

My process begins with one idea: I am not the user

01

Research & Observation

I begin by understanding the problem from the user’s perspective. I explore how people interact with similar systems, ask questions, and gather insights.

02

Define the Problem

I organize findings into a clear problem statement focused on user needs, not just features or assumptions.

03

Ideation

I brainstorm multiple solutions without limiting creativity too early, allowing unexpected ideas to emerge.

04

Prototype & Iterate

I create sketches or wireframes, test them with users or peers, and refine the design based on feedback.

04 · Design Philosophy

“I am not the user.”

This phrase shapes the way I approach design. It reminds me that a design solution should not be based only on my assumptions, preferences, or expectations. Instead, design should be grounded in how real users think, behave, and experience a product.

This philosophy helps me treat design as a continuous cycle of learning, testing, and refinement. Rather than seeing design as a single linear path, I see it as an ongoing conversation between ideas, users, and outcomes.

05 · Academic Learning and Influences

Connecting design practice with HCI research

Throughout the course, HCI readings helped me understand that effective design is not only about visual aesthetics. It also involves psychology, cognition, usability, accessibility, and the way people make decisions when interacting with technology.

These academic ideas helped me create a flexible design framework that values experimentation while maintaining a strong focus on user needs.

06 · Reflection

Growth as a designer

This project helped me realize that good design requires curiosity, patience, and openness to change. I became more comfortable starting projects by asking questions, observing users, and testing ideas instead of assuming I already understood the problem.

Ultimately, this project helped me develop a clearer identity as a designer who values empathy, research, iteration, and continuous improvement. These principles now guide how I approach creative and technical challenges in UX and interactive design.
