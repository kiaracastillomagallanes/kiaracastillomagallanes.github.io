01 · Project Overview

Redesigning Sakura-Con through UX strategy and brand identity

Sakura-Con is a large anime convention held annually in Seattle, bringing together fans of anime, gaming, cosplay, Japanese culture, panels, performances, exhibitions, and community experiences. Because the event includes a wide range of activities and information, the website plays an important role in helping attendees plan their visit.

The main challenge was not the lack of information, but the way information was structured and presented. The original website contained dense content, unclear hierarchy, and navigation patterns that made it difficult for users to quickly understand where to begin.

Problem

Information overload created confusion

The original website presented several usability challenges, especially for first-time attendees. Users struggled with navigating dense pages, identifying key information, and understanding the next step in the planning process.

Goal

Create a clearer and more guided experience

The goal was to improve navigation, usability, accessibility, and visual consistency while preserving Sakura-Con’s recognizable identity. The redesign focused on reducing cognitive load and helping users feel more confident while planning their convention experience.

02 · Research & Insights

Understanding user needs and pain points

The research process included problem space analysis, content review, usability testing, and needs identification. These methods helped define how users interact with convention websites and what challenges appear when they try to find schedules, registration, travel information, policies, and application details.

Navigation Confusion

Users needed clearer entry points for major tasks such as registration, schedule, travel, and policies.

High Cognitive Load

Long text blocks and limited visual hierarchy made the website feel overwhelming.

Inconsistent Visual System

Different layouts, colors, and button styles reduced trust and made pages harder to scan.

03 · User Journey

Mapping the before and after experience

I used user journey mapping to understand the full experience across research, discovery, planning, decision-making, and post-event stages. The before journey showed cognitive load and frustration, while the redesigned journey focused on clarity, emotional continuity, and confident decision-making.

04 · UX Strategy

Design decisions guided by research

01

Clear Navigation

Primary sections were reorganized around user tasks instead of forcing users to search through dense content.

02

Content Chunking

Long information blocks were broken into cards, summaries, and sections to improve scannability.

03

Visual Hierarchy

Typography, spacing, color, and section structure were used to help users identify important actions faster.

04

Storytelling

The redesign uses visual rhythm and brand elements to make the convention experience feel more engaging.

05 · Brand System

A refined visual identity for digital and promotional use

The brand system was created to support a consistent and recognizable experience across the website, booklet, poster, and promotional materials. The visual direction uses a soft pink palette, Poppins typography, logo variations, iconography, and merchandise mockups to show how the brand can extend across different touchpoints.

06 · Prototype

High-fidelity website prototype

The prototype redesigned the Sakura-Con website experience with stronger hierarchy, clearer content organization, more consistent UI components, and a more guided structure for users. The goal was to help attendees quickly find key information while keeping the experience visually connected to the convention identity.

07 · Poster Design

Extending the visual system into promotional media

The poster applies the redesigned brand identity in a promotional format. It uses color, typography, circular Japanese-inspired elements, and anime-style visual energy to communicate the Sakura-Con experience in a clear and engaging way.

08 · Reflection

What I learned

This project helped me understand that a strong UX/UI portfolio project should not only show final screens, but also explain the thinking behind the design. Through research, journey mapping, brand refinement, and prototyping, I learned how to connect usability decisions with visual storytelling.

The final redesign demonstrates how thoughtful structure, hierarchy, and branding can make a complex event website feel clearer, more accessible, and more engaging.
